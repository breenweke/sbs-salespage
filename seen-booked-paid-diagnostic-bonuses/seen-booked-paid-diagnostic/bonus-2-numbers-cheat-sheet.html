The Numbers That Matter
What to aim for, what it means when it is off, and the one thing to check first.
Before you read these benchmarks: every number below is a general guide — not a verdict on your business. A $12 CPL for a $5,000 service is excellent. The same CPL for a $97 offer is a problem. Use these ranges to spot when something is worth investigating, not to grade yourself. Context always wins over benchmarks.

Meta Ads Manager
Metric What to aim for When it is off — the signal First thing to check
Cost Per Lead Ads Manager → Results column
$2–$8Healthy
$8–$15Watch it
$15+Investigate
Lower ranges for broad audiences. Higher CPL is acceptable with a high-ticket offer or very warm targeting.
 Leads getting more expensive without a corresponding improvement in booking rate. Often appears alongside rising frequency. Check frequency first. If above 3.0 your audience has seen the ad enough times — refresh the creative before adjusting budget or targeting.
Click-Through Rate Ads Manager → CTR (link click-through rate)
1.5%+Strong
0.8–1.5%Acceptable
Under 0.8%Hook issue
Cold traffic often runs 0.5–1%. Do not panic below 1.5% if your CPL and booking rate are healthy.
 People see the ad but do not click. The impression count is climbing but leads are not. The ad is visible but not stopping anyone. Rewrite the first line. The hook needs to name the exact situation your ideal client is already in — not the solution you offer. Test one new hook before changing anything else.
Opt-In Rate Ads Manager → Landing page views vs. leads
30%+Strong
20–30%Acceptable
Under 20%Page issue
Warm traffic converts higher. Cold traffic at 25–30% is genuinely strong.
 CTR is healthy — people are clicking — but they land on the page and leave without opting in. The ad earned the click but the page lost the lead. Check message match. Does your page headline mirror the exact language in your ad? Any disconnect between ad and page creates hesitation that kills conversions.
Frequency Ads Manager → Frequency column (may need to add)
1.5–2.5Healthy
2.5–4.0Watch it
4.0+Refresh creative
Average times each person has seen your ad. Rising frequency alongside rising CPL = creative fatigue.
 CPL starts rising even though nothing in the campaign changed. The same people keep seeing the same ad and have already decided whether to act on it. Introduce a new creative alongside the existing one — do not turn the original off. Test the new version at a lower budget first. If it outperforms, scale it up and pause the original.
Booking platform — Calendly, GHL, Kartra, etc.
Metric What to aim for When it is off — the signal First thing to check
Booking Rate Calls booked ÷ total leads × 100 — calculated manually
15%+Strong
8–15%Developing
Under 8%Booked stage gap
This is the most important number in your funnel. A healthy booking rate with a high CPL is still a working system. A low booking rate with a great CPL is not.
 Leads are opting in consistently but the calendar is empty or filling only from people you manually reached out to. The top of the funnel is working. The middle is not. Check Email 1. Is it triggering automatically within 30 minutes of opt-in? If not — or if you do not have one — this is why. Build the follow-up sequence before touching anything else.
Show-Up Rate Calls completed ÷ calls booked × 100 — track manually or in CRM
70%+Strong
55–70%Acceptable
Under 55%Fix reminders
Cold traffic show-up rates of 55–65% are normal. Warm audience show-up rates should be 75%+. Do not compare cold and warm traffic show-up rates.
 People book calls and then do not show up. You sit in the Zoom room and wait. The booking happened — the infrastructure to protect it did not. Check your reminder emails. Does a confirmation go out immediately after booking? Does a reminder go out 24 hours before? If either is missing, set them up before the next call is booked.
Email platform — Kartra, ActiveCampaign, Mailchimp, etc.
Metric What to aim for When it is off — the signal First thing to check
Open Rate Email platform → campaign or sequence reports
35%+Strong
20–35%Acceptable
Under 20%Subject line issue
New subscriber sequences typically open higher. Broadcast emails to cold lists run lower. Compare sequences to sequences and broadcasts to broadcasts.
 Emails are being delivered but not opened. Your name is being recognized but your subject line is not earning the click. Or your emails are landing in spam or promotions. Send a test to yourself. Does it land in spam or promotions? If not — rewrite the subject line to name a specific situation rather than a general topic. Test one change at a time.
Click Rate Email platform → clicks ÷ opens (sometimes called CTOR)
10%+Strong
5–10%Acceptable
Under 5%CTA issue
Only applies to emails that contain a link or ask people to take action. Purely value emails with no CTA will naturally show low click rates — that is expected.
 People open the email but do not click through. The subject line worked — the body did not convert the open into action. Usually a CTA problem, not a content problem. Find your CTA and move it up. Most people never scroll far enough to see it. One link. One action. Visible without scrolling. Remove any competing links in the same email.
Unsubscribe Rate Email platform → unsubscribes ÷ delivered × 100
Under 0.3%Healthy
0.3–0.5%Watch it
0.5%+Relevance issue
Some unsubscribes after every email are healthy — self-selection keeps the list clean. A spike after one specific email almost always points to that email as the cause.
 More people are opting out than expected after a specific email or campaign. The content felt misaligned with why people signed up in the first place. Identify which email caused the spike. Review its content against the promise of your lead magnet. If the email veered from that problem, realign. Consistent relevance holds lists together.
If your Booked stage diagnostic score was below 60%: the booking rate is the only number worth focusing on right now. Everything else is secondary until leads are converting to calls. Fix the stage first — then use this cheat sheet to optimize within it.
