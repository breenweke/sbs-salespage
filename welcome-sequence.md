## Sequence structure notes

**Emotional arc (what each email is actually doing):**

| Day | Email | Emotional move |
|-----|-------|----------------|
| 0 | Delivery + frame | *"I trust her. Let me open the guide."* |
| 1 | Agitate | *"Oh. That's exactly what's happening to me."* |
| 2 | Quick win | *"She actually gave me something real. Not just hype."* |
| 3 | Offer intro | *"Wait, she builds this for you? I should look into that."* |
| 4 | Urgency | *"If I'm going to do this, now is the time."* |

**Why this works for Bree's audience:**
- No reply-word pattern (per your direction) — CTAs are direct links to install page OR calendar
- Every "problem" email references your real story ($544 → 16 calls) — credibility through experience, not claims
- Linda's story is the proof in email 4 (the decision email) — right place, right time
- 5-installs-a-month scarcity only surfaces in emails 4 & 5 — not used to manipulate, only to inform
- The guide is referenced repeatedly so readers who skimmed it initially get pulled back to it
- Tone is direct and calm throughout — no hype, no shaming, no urgency games

**If you want to test:** after 2-3 weeks running this sequence, you can track open rates per email and swap the ⭐ subject line with a challenger from the other options to A/B test.
