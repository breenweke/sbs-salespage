About Bree

Nine years of paid-ads pattern recognition, lived experience, and stewardship-first entrepreneurship.

Bree Nweke is the founder of Perfectly Made Marketing and the creator of The Self-Booking System — a Meta-ads-driven methodology that helps established service providers build a calendar that fills itself, without posting daily, chasing leads in DMs, or being the engine of their own business.

She began her career in 2016 as a single mom selling life insurance, where she discovered Meta ads as the only way to reach her ideal clients during the hours she could not be in front of them. After years of refining what she now calls the Self-Booking System, she has personally trained 81 students through her courses and built done-for-you installs for life insurance agents, mortgage brokers, business funding experts, psychologists, therapists, and faith-led coaches and consultants.

Bree speaks with the calm directness of someone who has done the work — no hype, no shaming, no marketing-bro energy. She brings nine years of paid-ads pattern recognition, the lived experience of building a business that supports a family, and a stewardship-first approach to entrepreneurship that resonates with audiences who care as much about how they grow as what they grow.

Bree loves long road trips with her family, unhurried mornings, and unapologetically deep conversations about why most marketing advice is either wrong or incomplete.

Topic 03

You Were Not Built to Be the Engine: The Mindset Shift Every Service Provider Needs

"Most service providers hit a ceiling because they're running on themselves — their hours, their energy, their consistency. I want to walk your audience through the mindset shift (and the system) that turns a service business from a 'high-paying job' into something that runs without you being the engine."

What we'll cover

  • Why service providers consistently get stuck at $5K-$25K/month — the structural reason, not a hustle reason
  • The "high-paying job" trap: the moment your business secretly stops being a business
  • The four private thoughts that signal you're hitting this wall: "I should be further along by now" / "If I take a week off, my income disappears" / "I'm tired of starting every month from zero" / "Why is this still this hard after all these years?"
  • What it actually looks like to stop being the engine — practical, not philosophical
  • The role of paid traffic and an email list in becoming the steward of your business instead of its operator
Audience Takeaway

Listeners walk away seeing their business through a different lens — and feeling permission to stop being the engine, with a clear sense of what should replace them.

Best For

Faith-led entrepreneur podcasts (your strongest topic for that audience) · Women in business podcasts (especially serving second-career founders) · Service provider podcasts (therapists, coaches, consultants) · Mid-career professional podcasts · Lifestyle & family-aligned business podcasts

Topic 05

Why Your Email List Is Worth More Than Your Booked Calls

"Most service providers obsess over booked calls and ignore the asset that compounds for the next 10 years — their email list. I want to walk your audience through how to build an email list as a byproduct of your booking system, why it changes everything during slow months, and what most marketers won't tell you about owning vs renting your audience."

What we'll cover

  • Why booked calls are short-term wins — and email lists are long-term assets
  • The compounding math: how every campaign you run adds to a list that produces forever
  • Why organic alone (Instagram, TikTok, posting) cannot build the list at any speed your business can wait for
  • The "every ad dollar does two jobs" framework: fills your calendar this week + grows your list for every week after
  • What to do with your list once you have it — broadcasts, slow-month rescues, new offer launches, repeat-client touchpoints
Audience Takeaway

Listeners walk away thinking about their marketing in 10-year terms instead of 30-day terms — and seeing their list as the actual business they're building.

Best For

Marketing & business growth podcasts · Solopreneur podcasts · Online course creator podcasts (their audiences live and die by lists) · Email marketing podcasts (Bree as the "system before the email" angle) · Wealth-mindset business podcasts
